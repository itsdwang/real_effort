Instructions

This game combines elements of the Public Goods game and Real Effort Transcription task into one activity. In this game, there are two modes: no transcription and transcription, both of which are described as follows:

Mode 1 (No Transcription): This is the Public Goods game modified slightly. You will be in a randomly formed group of {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants, where each participant is given a fixed income, and a tax of X% is automatically collected. You must decide how much of your income you will report. Reported income could be any integer from 0 to your base income.

Mode 2 (Transcription): You do not start with a fixed income. Rather, you are shown two images of incomprehensible text and are required to transcribe (copy) the text into a text entry field. The quality of a subject's transcription is measured by the Levenshtein distance. The accuracy of each players' transcription of the second image ultimately determines each person's income. The remainder of mode 2 follows mode 1.

The earnings from the project are calculated as follows: The tax collected from each of the {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants is added up and multiplied by a certain factor, and the resulting amount is the total earnings from the project, which is evenly split among all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Each participant's payoff equals the their share of the tax money plus the amount of income they had remaining after tax was deducted.
